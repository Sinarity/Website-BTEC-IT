The World Wildlife Fund

The World Wildlife fund is one of the world's major wildlife conservation charities that have been operating for over 60 years. The WWF strives to help communities and the world understand what is needed in order to preserve animals and save them from extinction. The WWF also helps humans and environments at the same time. Because of this, the WWF is one of the most respected funds that is currently working for the better of the Earth. They are currently working in over 100 countries around the world.





Sheldrick Wildlife Trust

The Sheldrick Wildlife Trust is a wildlife conservation charity that stives to protect endangered species within the heart of the African country, Kenya. They make campaign against poaching, and safeguarding the natural environment. Their main job is to make sure that poachers do not harm rare species of elephants and rhinos that are endangered or vulnerable. The fundraiser was established in 1977 and for 43 years, have been protecting the Kenyan savannas and wildlife. The SWT is known to be one of the largest operating funds in Kenyas, being supported by both the Kenyan and the British governments.

The Sea Turtle Foundation

The Sea Turtle Foundation is an Australian non-profit and non-governmental organization focused on protecting sea turtles through research, education and action. The STF has been working to address the effects of climate change and hunting and have strived to make an attempt to save as many turtles in Queensland as posssible.
