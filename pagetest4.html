The Pangolin

  • The Pangolin's name comes from the Malay word 'pengguling', which means 'rolling up'. This is a reference to the animal's defense tactic of 'rolling up' into a ball.
  • Like anteaters, Pangolins have no teeth, and no jaw. They only have a long sticky tongue that can extend up to 40cm.
  • Panglins are solitary animals. This means that they mostly spend their time hunting and their daily routines alone. The only time when they do get together is when they go and mate for reproduction.

The Giant Tortoise

  • In 2012, the last remaining Giant Galapagos Tortoise of the pinto island died at the age of 102 years old.
  • The Giant Tortoise seem to have the ability to live without water or food for a little more than a year. This is impressive compared to the human's limit of three days without water and two weeks without food.
  • Despite popular beliefs, and interpretations in cartoons, the giant tortoises' shell is actually a part of their skeleton.

The Giant Panda

  • Unlike their cousins, pandas have unique vertical slits for eyes instead, the eyes are very similar to those belonging to domesticated cats.
  • During a panda's first month, the mother can be very protective, and keep contact with them almost 100% of the time, with the cub resting on their front and the remaining areas covered by the head, paw or arm.
  • Pandas have a padded wrist bone that helps them grip bamboo easily and act like a thumb. Other bears have this feature too, but pandas have theirs significantly longer, making this feature unique to them.
  • Pandas spend around 10-16 hours a day eating, compared to a human's 1-3 hours of eating time.
  • Pandas have this unique and odd behavior of marking their territory. A panda sometimes would go into a handstand in order to urinate on the higher areas of a tree, effectivly marking their scent and rendering it hard, or impossible for other animals to mark their scent.

The Polar Bear

  • Despite looking white, a polar bear's skin is actually black, which the fur being translucent. The only reason the fur looks white is due to the translucency and the snow reflecting off the fur, giving the polar bear their unique look.
  • Since polar bears spend most of their time at sea, they are classified as marine animals. In fact, they are the only bear species to be classified as a marine animal.
  • Even though a polar bear's daily routine is mostly hunting, avout 2% of them are actually successful. Because of this, polar bears have managed to scavenge off carcasses of other successful feedings as well as vegetation and small mammals.
